# Will the Customer Accept the Coupon?

## 1. General Overview

The strongest pattern across all coupons:  
**People are much more likely to accept a coupon for a place they already go to regularly.**

## 2. Bar Coupon Findings

Key differences between acceptors and non-acceptors:

- **Visit frequency is huge**  
  - People who go to bars **3** or **fewer times a month** → very low acceptance (~37%)  
  - People who go **4 times a month or more** → much higher acceptance (~77%)  
  → If bars aren’t already part of their routine, they usually skip the coupon.

- **Passenger matters a lot**  
  - **Alone**, **with partner**, or **with friends** → high acceptance (~71%) 
  - **With kids in the car** → acceptance drops sharply (~38%)

- **Other notes**  
  - Younger drivers (under ~30) and people with certain occupations sometimes showed slightly higher rates, but the biggest driver was still **how often they normally visit bars**.

## 3. Coffee House (Cafe) Coupon Findings

Coffee house coupons were accepted (~49%) more often than bar coupons (~41%), but still showed clear patterns.

Key differences:

- **Visit frequency again dominates**  
  - People who go to coffee houses **never** or **less than once a month** → lower acceptance (~35%)
  - People who visit **1–3 times/month or more** → noticeably higher acceptance rates (66%)
  → Same story as bars: if coffee shops are already part of their life, they’re way more likely to say yes.

- **Age impact is large**  
  - **Under 21** years old → hige rise in acceptance (~70%)
  - **26 years and above** → lower acceptance, closer to total cafe average rate (~49%)

- **Other notes**  
  - Men sometimes accepted coffee house coupons at slightly higher rates than women (small difference)  
  - Income showed showed an interesting trend where the lowest and highest incomes had similar acceptances, but there was a dip in acceptances in the middle imcome ranges
  - Occupation had an impact where jobs with longer or unusual hours (healthcare, maintenance) had higher acceptance rates
